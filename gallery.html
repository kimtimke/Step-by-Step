Discover the Walkita Concept

Introduction

A Walkita is a 2-3 hour Walk & Talk session that takes place outdoors, designed to achieve specific objectives and outcomes. This unique concept provides a refreshing and relaxed yet focused setting for contemplating business processes and areas for improvement. Participants have the freedom to discuss topics they deem important, ranging from challenges and opportunities to financial performance, customers, and strategy.

Key Elements of Walkita

  • Empowering individuals to achieve more than they thought possible
  • Tools acquired from mentorship programs for handling stress in managing director role
  • Clearing the mind through long walks by the sea or riverside
  • Refreshing perspective by moving discussions outdoors
  • Developing attentive listening skills for effective skill development
  • Fusion of exercise, mental stimulation, and personal circumstances

Walkita Session Structure

Multiple Walkita sessions are organized throughout the intervention, starting with an introductory session to familiarize participants with the topics. Subsequent sessions delve into specific subjects and may involve setting up a whiteboard on the beach.

Addressing Challenges

Walkita sessions tackle challenging questions related to business performance and profitability, encouraging participants to explore opportunities for improvement. Difficult questions may include why good performance isn't great, why only break-even and not profit, or how to generate future profits for SMEs.

Enjoyable Elements

Walkita sessions may incorporate enjoyable elements like bag pick-ups or photo competitions, fostering a relaxed and engaging atmosphere during the discussions.

Outcome and Action Plans

The key outcomes of Walkita discussions are summarized in a concise one or two-page "OUTPUT Sheet," which serves as the foundation for actionable plans and next steps following the attentive conversation.
